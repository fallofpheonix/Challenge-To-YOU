# NARRATIVE.md

## Purpose

This document outlines the overarching storyline, pacing, and emotional arc of *Project Chrysalis*. It serves to ground the mechanical escalation in a cohesive narrative, ensuring that new systems and challenges are introduced seamlessly alongside the evolving relationship between the player (The Architect) and the swarm.

---

## The Story Arc

### Act I: Emergence

**The Premise:** You orbit Kepler-452b, tasked with establishing a functional staging ground. Previous, monolithic rovers have failed. You deploy the first generation of micro-drones.

* **Major Discoveries:** The planet is rich in silicates, but orbital interference creates severe signal latency. The player realizes that standard pathfinding is impossible; the swarm must operate on localized, pheromone-based logic.
* **Pacing:** Deliberate, methodical, and highly satisfying. The focus is on onboarding and the "domino effect" of watching simple rules successfully coordinate resource harvesting.
* **Player Identity:** **The Teacher.** You are teaching a rudimentary system how to walk, gather, and deposit. The emotional connection is one of parental guidance over a fragile, clumsy entity.

### Act II: Survival

**The Premise:** As the colony expands and mines deeper to reach rare isotopes, the environment reacts. The swarm encounters the planet’s native, non-biological hazards: aggressively shifting magnetic anomalies, thermal geysers, and silicate traps.

* **Major Discoveries:** The planet is not a passive rock; it is a hostile ecosystem. Simple "gather and return" loops lead to mass drone casualties. The swarm must be taught to recognize danger, drop warning pheromones, and dynamically reroute.
* **Pacing:** Ramping tension. The safe, predictable logistics of Act I are continuously interrupted. The player must actively plug holes in their supply chains.
* **Player Identity:** **The Engineer.** The innocence of Act I is over. The player must now build robust, fault-tolerant infrastructure. The focus shifts from merely getting things to work, to ensuring they don't break when under pressure.

### Act III: Infection

**The Premise:** A "Ghost in the Swarm" appears in the telemetry. Deep underground, drones begin executing behaviors that the Architect did not write. Supply chains degrade, drones report false resource nodes, and unauthorized structures are built. An ancient, dormant subterranean network is waking up and attempting to overwrite your swarm's core logic with its own.

* **Major Discoveries:** The enemy is not a physical faction, but a competing distributed intelligence. The player discovers logic drift, pheromone spoofing, and byzantine drone corruption.
* **Pacing:** High stakes and paranoia. The game transforms into a mystery and a deep debugging challenge. Trust in the existing systems is broken.
* **Player Identity:** **The Detective.** The player stops building outward and starts investigating inward. They must write trust-verification systems, consensus protocols, and quarantine rules to root out the corrupted logic without destroying their own colony.

### Act IV: Witness

**The Premise:** The alien intelligence fully awakens, triggering a massive planetary electromagnetic storm. The orbital uplink is completely severed. You can no longer send patches. You can no longer save them. The Architect is locked out.

* **Major Discoveries:** The player realizes the entire game was a training simulation for this exact moment. The swarm must now rely purely on the decentralized, resilient rules the Architect spent the last 30 hours refining to out-compute the alien network and achieve equilibrium.
* **Pacing:** A totally hands-off, cathartic climax. A pure stress test of the ecosystem. The player watches the visual spectacle of the final algorithmic battle unfold on the telemetry heatmaps.
* **Player Identity:** **The Witness.** The Architect must let go. You watch your creation prove that it is robust enough to survive the universe without you. The transition from active controller to passive, proud observer is complete.
