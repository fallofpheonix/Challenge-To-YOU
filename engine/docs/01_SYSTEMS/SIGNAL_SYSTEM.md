# SIGNAL_SYSTEM.md

## Purpose

Digital pheromones are the defining identity of *Project Chrysalis*. Because drones lack global pathfinding (no A* algorithms) and direct communication, the entire logistics network relies on this decentralized, environment-based signaling system. This document defines how information is physically painted onto the world and how it behaves over time.

## Signal Types

Signals are discrete data markers dropped onto grid coordinates.

* **Resource_Gradient:** Indicates a path toward an active mining node.
* **Return_Vector:** Indicates a path back to the nearest operational Hub.
* **Hazard_Marker:** A localized warning indicating recent drone destruction or active environmental threats.
* **Construction_Tag:** Marks a blueprint requiring material deposits.
* **Spoofed_Signature (Act III):** Malicious markers generated by the Alien Network that perfectly mimic `Resource_Gradient` or `Return_Vector` signals to lure drones into dead zones.

## Decay (Evaporation)

To prevent the grid from becoming permanently saturated with obsolete data, signals are highly ephemeral.

* **The Decay Rule:** During Phase 2 of the simulation tick, every signal on the grid has its intensity reduced by a fixed integer value.
* **Variable Rates:** Different signal types decay at different rates. A `Hazard_Marker` persists significantly longer than a `Resource_Gradient`, ensuring danger zones remain flagged even if traffic temporarily stops.
* **Wipe:** Once a signal's intensity drops to `0`, it is completely wiped from the grid's memory arrays.

## Propagation (Diffusion)

Signals do not just sit on a single tile; they bleed outward to create navigable paths.

* **Gradient Formation:** When a signal is dropped on a cell at high intensity (e.g., 255), the simulation engine automatically diffuses a fraction of that intensity to adjacent cells during the next tick.
* **The Breadcrumb Trail:** As a drone carries a silicate back to the Hub, it continually drops `Return_Vector` signals. Because earlier drops have decayed more than recent drops, this creates a natural "uphill" intensity gradient that other drones can follow perfectly to the source.

## Range

A drone's sensor suite is intentionally myopic to force emergent swarm behavior.

* **The 3x3 Limitation:** A drone can only read the signal data of its current tile and the 8 immediately adjacent tiles (Moore neighborhood).
* **Gradient Descent/Ascent:** A drone programmed to `FOLLOW Resource_Gradient` will simply scan its 8 adjacent tiles, identify the tile with the highest integer value for that specific signal, and step onto it.

## Conflict Rules

Because multiple signals will inevitably overlap in high-traffic sectors, strict conflict resolution rules apply.

* **Stacking (Same Type):** If multiple drones drop a `Resource_Gradient` on the same tile, the intensities are added together, capped at a hard maximum (e.g., 255). This means high-traffic highways have a much stronger "gravitational pull" than a single scout's trail.
* **Coexistence (Different Types):** A single grid cell can hold multiple different signal types simultaneously (e.g., a cell can have a `Return_Vector` of 120 and a `Hazard_Marker` of 50).
* **Execution Priority:** The grid itself does not prioritize signals; it simply stores them. Priority is dictated entirely by the Architect's code. The player must write the hierarchy (e.g., `IF Hazard_Marker > 10 THEN Evade ELSE IF Resource_Gradient > 0 THEN Follow`).
* **The Alien Conflict:** When Alien nodes drop `Spoofed_Signatures`, they intentionally stack them on top of valid paths to override the Architect's gradients. Conflict resolution requires the player to research and program Cryptographic Signing, teaching drones to ignore signals that lack the Architect's specific integer handshake.
